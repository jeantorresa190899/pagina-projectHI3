Background

1Origins

The city does not appear until after the Roman conquest. Roman rule stretched from the 1st century AD. until the 5th century, when the Empire fell. In the 3rd century, Londinium, with its port, was an important population center. Ruined by the Anglo-Saxon invasions of the 5th century, in the 7th century it became the capital of the small kingdom of Essex and was the episcopal seat. Although in the 9th century it suffered Scandinavian incursions, the establishment of Danish settlers in the neighborhood fostered entrepreneurship and a desire for commerce, which made it the first urban center in the country. Its wealth attracted Scandinavian and Danish kings who besieged the city and forced it to pay tribute. From 1067 the city had the same rights as a county and only depended on the royal authority. The Tower of London dates from this time. In 1191 the city was constituted as a municipality (corporation) when a mayor replaced the prime minister (portreeve). In 1215, London had the privilege of electing its mayor each year.

London has a temperate oceanic climate, similar to the rest of southern Britain. Despite its reputation as a rainy city, London receives less rainfall in a year than Rome or Bordeaux .110 Winters are cold, with about two frosts per week in the suburbs between November and March. Snow falls rarely, about four or five times a year between December and February, while in the months of March and April it can snow once every two or three years. Winter temperatures range from −4 ° C to 14 ° C
